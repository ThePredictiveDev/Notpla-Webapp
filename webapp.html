The Core Technology

Notpla's innovation lies in transforming brown seaweed into a versatile biopolymer. The performance hinges on alginate, a natural polysaccharide whose unique properties enable the creation of strong, flexible, and fully compostable materials.

Alginate Films: The "Egg-Box" Model

The foundation of Notpla's films and coatings is a process of ionic cross-linking. Guluronic acid blocks (G-blocks) within the alginate chains link together using calcium ions, forming a strong and stable 3D hydrogel network. This is known as the "egg-box" model.

Ooho & Spherification

For liquid encapsulation, Notpla utilizes spherification, a technique where a liquid containing sodium alginate is exposed to a calcium salt solution. This triggers an immediate reaction, forming a gel-like skin around the liquid core. The thickness can be tuned for different applications.

    A base liquid is mixed with sodium alginate, a salt derived from seaweed.

  • 2

  • 3

Commercial Products

From edible liquid pods to plastic-free takeaway containers, Notpla's technology has been translated into a range of groundbreaking commercial products that directly replace single-use plastics.

Ooho Edible Pods

Flexible pods for liquids like water, sports drinks, and sauces. Ideal for events like marathons, replacing plastic cups and bottles.

Category: Edible

Coated Takeaway Boxes

Cardboard and fiber boxes with a grease- and water-resistant seaweed coating. A plastic-free, PFAS-free alternative that is fully home compostable.

Category: Coatings

Notpla Film

A transparent, heat-sealable film for dry goods sachets or as a component in other packaging. Some versions are designed to dissolve in water.

Category: Films

Oil & Sauce Pipettes

Single-dose, edible pipettes for oils and dressings, eliminating the need for plastic sauce sachets in meal kits and takeaways.

Category: Edible

Notpla Paper

Paper made with 30% seaweed byproduct, reducing wood pulp demand and utilizing the entire harvest. It's naturally recyclable.

Category: Coatings

Water-Soluble Sachets

Films designed for home care products like laundry pods or bath oils that dissolve completely upon contact with water, leaving no residue.

Advantages & Environmental Impact

Notpla's products offer significant environmental advantages over existing solutions, from a lower carbon footprint to a truly circular end-of-life.

Lower Carbon Footprint

A Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) shows that Notpla's seaweed-coated packaging has drastically lower embodied CO‚ÇÇ equivalent emissions compared to conventional plastic and PLA-coated alternatives.

Rapid Biodegradation

Notpla products biodegrade in natural environments within 4-6 weeks, similar to a piece of fruit. Plastic bottles can take over 450 years.

Home Compostable

Unlike many bioplastics (like PLA) that require industrial composting facilities, Notpla's materials break down in a standard home compost bin.

No Microplastics

Because it's a natural material, Notpla decomposes fully without leaving behind harmful microplastics that pollute ecosystems.

Non-Toxic & PFAS-Free

The coatings are free from per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS), often called "forever chemicals," which are common in grease-proof paper packaging.

Reduces Fossil Fuel Reliance

By using a renewable, nature-based feedstock, Notpla helps move the packaging industry away from its dependence on finite fossil fuels.

Simplified Waste Streams

The seaweed-coated boxes can be recycled with paper or composted, eliminating consumer confusion and reducing contamination in recycling bins.
